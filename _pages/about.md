I am a dual-major Ph.D. candidate in **Computer Science** and
**Biosystems and Agricultural Engineering** at
[**Michigan State University**](https://msu.edu/). I work with
Dr. Amirpouyan Nejadhashemi and Dr. Pang-Ning Tan.

My research lies at the intersection of **deep learning, computer vision,
spatiotemporal modeling, hydroinformatics, and environmental science**.
I develop scalable machine-learning methods for understanding and predicting
complex Earth and hydrologic systems.

My current work focuses on basin-scale representation learning, distributed
streamflow modeling, prediction in ungauged basins, and transferable
spatiotemporal models. I am particularly interested in self-supervised
learning, foundation models, multimodal learning, scientific time-series
forecasting, and interpretable AI.

Before beginning my Ph.D., I worked as a **Machine Learning Engineer at
ibex**, where I developed large-scale computer-vision and speech-processing
systems for real-time compliance monitoring. I previously worked as a
Research Assistant at the Al-Khwarizmi Institute of Computer Science,
University of Engineering and Technology Lahore, on efficient object
detection and explainable AI for medical imaging.

I am open to research collaborations and opportunities involving
**AI for science, Earth-system modeling, geospatial machine learning,
hydrology, and scientific foundation models**.


## Research interests

- Machine learning for hydrology and environmental science
- Spatiotemporal representation learning
- Scientific and geospatial foundation models
- Self-supervised and multimodal learning
- Computer vision and remote sensing
- Scientific time-series forecasting
- Interpretable artificial intelligence
- Optimization for environmental decision-making
